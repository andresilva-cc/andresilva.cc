The /about Facts "interests" cell shipped as a three-item, ` · `-delimited list: `agentic workflows · user-facing AI · developer tooling`.

**Why:** Two distinct AI senses are kept apart on purpose — (1) using AI to BUILD apps = `agentic workflows`; (2) AI as a user-facing FEATURE shipped INTO products = `user-facing AI`. An earlier draft blurred them by giving both items the same leading token ("AI..."), which read as redundant. The two AI items must diverge at the leading token, and the feature-sense item must not drift toward a market-category label that ages with the hype cycle (rejected: `LLM products`, `AI-native products`, `AI products`, etc.). "user-facing" works because it names WHO meets the model, fencing the build-vs-feature axis in the wording itself.

**How to apply:**
- Keep the items as bare lowercase noun phrases, ` · `-delimited, no terminal punctuation, peer vocabulary (no buzzword-adjacent words like "intelligent").
- Never swap `agentic workflows`.
- The two AI items are a deliberate build→ship pair and stay adjacent in that internal order (`agentic workflows` then `user-facing AI`) — this is the hard constraint.
- Do NOT reorder the line. The cell key is `interests` — it claims CURRENT focus, not tenure, so it leads with current AI work; `developer tooling` closes the triplet as the durable foundation. The Facts grid has no oldest-first house convention (the `languages` row is ordered by proficiency), so do not resurrect a chronology argument to justify reordering.
- `LLM` stays uppercase if ever used (initialism).
